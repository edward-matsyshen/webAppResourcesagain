    All About Responsive Design
    This 16 course site is a wonderful encyclopedia of tips and information related to responsive design and how to implement it with images, icons, layouts etc. The courses also provide a video element as well, which is awesome! Showing end results and the step-by-steps of implemeting the "srcset" and "size" attribute. This is a great article (I don't know if I would classify this as an article, tutorial, or course) and I will efinanatly look back at this.

    Integrating Databases
    This site shows the "down and dirty" details of integrating your own database to your site. Fairly useful and a great refresher to me as I haven't really integrated databases to any website since PROG 110. I put this page here since out future project will have you design a website that implements API and databases. I will have to reference this and mutliple other sites to see how to properly implement pre-existing data to the project.

    CSS Grids
    Goes in depth with dynamic layouts and image grids for your site. Personally, I believe that grids can make a huge difference in making your site look more polished and cleaner than your code appears. Since one of my ideas for a website for this course is to make a Home-Depot-like website, the use of grid for images is especially important to learn how to properly implement.

    Design for Devs
    Tania Rascia writes some great articles about everything related to Application Development and the basics and complexities of using certain programming. If it wasn't for the Responsive Web Discussion I would have probably never found this article. She talks about the step by step proccess of creating a site, choosing waht design to focus on, and to manage trying to finish creating a site while tasking with structuring the site's layout, padding, hieght, colors, etc. She talks about writing your content first then worrying about design and not doing the opposite. She lists her guidelines/tips for the design she likes to use for her sites. I've actaully used a lot of her tips and reccomendations for creating this very page.
